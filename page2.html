Diet and Exercise Plan



Diet

The most important task that I want our of this wellness journey is to adopt healthier eating habits. Here's my plan for right now:



  1. Add more whole foods into my meals
  2. Eat foods based around what I need, not how I feel.
  3. Avoid the 'food noise' and focus on sustainability.



These goals I have for myself feels acheiveable. I will give myself grace, but I will need more details to visualize how to make it happen. So, here's what I got:

  1. Add more whole foods into my meals

    • Explore recipies that fit my budget range, while having whole foods.
    • Add fruits as a snack.
    • Find vegetable replacers for things like pasta.
    • Focus more on healthy carbs, like sweet potatoes

  2. Eat foods based around what I need, not how I feel.

    • Plan meals around my nutritional needs.
    • Focus on protein and fiber.
    • Research foods that have vitamins that I need.

  3. Avoid the 'food noise' and focus on sustainability.

    • Focus more on the nutritional value of foods.
    • Limit processed foods and focus on whole ingredients.
    • Eat filling meals that are also nutrient dense.

Challenges:

With these goals fully laid out, I can picture all the steps needed for a healthy diet. Nobody is perfect, I have to remind myself about that. Here's some things that I struggle with:



  1. Cooking after a long day of work.
  2. Forgetting my lunch at home.
  3. DoorDash TM and UberEats TM

Here's a plan that I've come up with to overcome these challenges:

  1. Cooking after a long day of work.

    • Prepping all 3 meals in advance. I've tried it before, and I feel that that was the best way to go.

  2. Forgetting my lunch at home.

    • I live pretty close to my work, so if I forget my lunch, I can just simply go and get it.

  3. DoorDash TM and UberEats TM

    • Limit the use of delivery services. But if I find myself ordering, I will order from places that have healthier options.

This is my diet plan that I think is sustainable for me. It's built to give me some grace, while also challenging me. It fruits the best for my lifestyle.

Exercise

I find my self going from one workout plan to the next. I never stick with it if I find it too difficult. I've done some research And expirements, and here's what I think will work for me:



  1. Walk 10,000 steps a day
  2. Do 20 minutes of Barre every weekday
  3. Do 10 minutes of core workouts every weekday

These goals seem to look difficult to achieve. But with an effective schedule, it can be possible. I will lay out my schedule:

  1. Walk 10,000 steps a day

    • Walk 3,000 steps in the morning
    • Walk 2,000 steps during work
    • Walk 2,000 steps during lunch
    • Walk 3,000 steps in the evening

  2. Do 20 minutes of Barre every weekday

    • Every morning, pull up a 20 minute full body barre workout video

  3. Do 10 minutes of core workouts every weekday

    • Every evening, pull up a 10 minute core workout video

The main reason why I decided on these specific workouts is because they are all focused on form. I want to improve my posture and balance. I work at an office job, so doing workouts that centers my core and posture will help me the most.

This workout plan is more difficult to keep up with. This is why I have a set schedule to make it easier to follow. I will also help with building a routine.
